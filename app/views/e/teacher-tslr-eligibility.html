Eligibility

To claim back student loan payments made through your wages between {{ globals.tax_year.start_date }} and {{ globals.tax_year.end_date }} (the last tax year), you must:

  • have qualified as a teacher after {{ globals.eligibility_criteria.qualified_date }}
  • still be employed at a school in the UK
  • have been employed at a state-funded school or academy, or in a 'special school' (a school with pupils who have special educational needs) in the last tax year
  • have been employed at a school that’s in a participating local authority area in England in the last tax year
  • have spent most of your time at the school teaching 11 to 16 year olds or, if you were on leave or off sick, have been employed to teach this age range

If you worked in a state-funded school or academy you must also have taught, or been employed to teach, at least one of these subjects for most of the last tax year:

  • biology
  • chemistry
  • computer science
  • physics
  • languages (not English)

If you worked in a special school, you do not need to have taught these subjects.

Participating local authority areas

You can only claim your student loan payments back in certain areas in England – areas where there are fewer teachers in these subjects at the moment. To make a claim, you must have taught at a school or academy in one of these local authority areas:

  • Barnsley
  • Blackpool
  • Bracknell Forest
  • Bradford
  • Cambridgeshire
  • Derby
  • Derbyshire
  • Doncaster
  • Halton
  • Knowsley
  • Luton
  • Middlesbrough
  • Norfolk
  • North-east Lincolnshire
  • North Yorkshire
  • Northamptonshire
  • Northumberland
  • Oldham
  • Peterborough
  • Portsmouth
  • Salford
  • Sefton
  • St Helens
  • Stoke-on-Trent
  • Suffolk

Effect on other benefits

If you're claiming benefits that need you to declare your income, you must include any payment you get from this service in that declaration.
